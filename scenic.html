The Great Wall

The Great Wall is one of China’s most iconic landmarks and a major reason many visitors travel to Beijing. The sections near the city offer a dramatic mountain landscape, a sense of scale, and a strong connection to the country’s long military history. Walking along the wall gives travelers a clear idea of why this site remains one of the most admired monuments in the world. The Forbidden City

Located in the heart of Beijing, the Forbidden City is a masterpiece of imperial architecture and one of the city’s most important heritage sites. Its vast courtyards, red walls, gold roofs, and carefully arranged halls show the ceremonial power of ancient Chinese rule. A visit here offers a deeper understanding of Beijing’s role as a political and cultural center.
