Official Introduction

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where imperial legacy and modern development exist side by side. For centuries, it has served as the political and cultural center of the country, and this long history can still be seen in its palace architecture, temple grounds, courtyard homes, and carefully preserved heritage spaces. Beijing has served as China’s capital for a very long time
Beijing has been the capital of several Chinese dynasties and later became the political center of modern China. This long history is reflected in the city’s grand palaces, old city layout, and many preserved heritage sites.
The Forbidden City is one of the world’s best-known palace complexes
The Forbidden City was once home to emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Today, it remains one of Beijing’s most important landmarks and gives visitors a clear sense of traditional Chinese court life, architecture, and ceremonial culture.
Beijing is famous for both tradition and modern energy
The city is known not only for history, but also for fast transport, busy commercial districts, creative neighborhoods, and active street life. That balance makes Beijing feel layered, lively, and constantly changing.
Beijing roast duck is one of the city’s signature dishes
Roast duck is often considered the most famous dish associated with Beijing. The combination of crisp skin, tender meat, and careful preparation has made it a symbol of the city’s refined food culture for generations.
